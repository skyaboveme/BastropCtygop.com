The primaries are behind us. The runoffs are settled. Now comes the main event: the **2026 General Election on November 3**. This is the race that determines who actually holds office — from the Governor's mansion to the Bastrop County Courthouse.

Here's everything you need to know to be prepared, informed, and effective as a conservative voter in Bastrop County.

### Key Dates to Remember

| Date | What |
|------|------|
| **October 3** | Last day to register to vote |
| **October 19–30** | Early voting period |
| **November 3** | Election Day – polls open 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM |

If you're not sure you're registered, check now at [VoteTexas.gov](https://www.votetexas.gov). Don't wait until October.

### What's on the Ballot

The 2026 cycle features critical races at every level:

- **Governor**: The top of the ticket sets the tone for the entire state.
- **Lieutenant Governor, Attorney General, Comptroller**: These statewide offices shape policy on school choice, border security, and fiscal accountability.
- **State Legislature**: Your state representative and state senator vote on property taxes, education funding, and gun rights — every session.
- **County Races**: Commissioners, judges, and other local offices that directly impact your roads, your taxes, and your courtrooms.

### Why Bastrop County Matters

Bastrop County is a battleground. Growth is bringing in new residents from Austin, Houston, and out of state. Some share our values. Some don't. The only way to hold the line is to **turn out every single Republican voter** — especially in the precincts where races are decided by dozens or hundreds of votes, not thousands.

In the 2024 general election, several local races in Bastrop County were decided by margins of less than 5%. Every vote counted. Every vote will count again.

### How to Maximize Your Impact

1. **Vote early.** Early voting gives you flexibility and reduces Election Day congestion. Find locations at our [Voter Hub](https://www.bastropctygop.com/resources/voter-hub/).
2. **Bring five people.** The single most effective thing any voter can do is bring friends, family, and neighbors to the polls. Make a list of five people and commit to getting them there.
3. **Volunteer.** Poll watchers, phone bankers, and block walkers are the backbone of every winning campaign. [Sign up to volunteer](https://www.bastropctygop.com/resources/volunteer/).
4. **Know your precinct chair.** Your [Precinct Chair](https://www.bastropctygop.com/about/precinct-chairs/) is your local point of contact for election information and volunteer coordination.

### The Stakes Are Real

This isn't a theoretical exercise. The people elected on November 3 will make decisions about your property taxes, your children's schools, your water, your roads, and your rights. If conservatives don't show up, someone else will make those decisions for us.

Mark the date. Make your plan. And bring your neighbors.
